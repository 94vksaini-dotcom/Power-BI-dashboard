# HR Attrition Dashboard | Power BI

## Dashboard Overview
This Power BI dashboard provides a complete analysis of employee attrition using HR data. 
It helps understand attrition trends based on department, age, gender, job role, education, salary, business travel, marital status, and overtime.

---

## Key KPIs
- Total Employees: 1470
- Active Employees: 1233
- Attrition Count: 237
- Overall Attrition Rate: 16%
- Male Employees: 882
- Female Employees: 588
- Average Employee Age: 37

- HR Attrition Rate: 19%

## Dashboard Analysis

### Attrition by Education Field & Department
- Highest attrition observed in **Life Sciences** and **Medical** education fields
- Sales and R&D departments contribute more to attrition compared to HR

### Employee Count by Age Group & Gender
- Majority of employees fall in the **26–35** and **36–45** age groups
- Male employee count is higher across most age groups

### Attrition % by Years in Current Role
- Higher attrition seen in employees with fewer years in the current role
- Attrition decreases as experience in the same role increases

### Attrition % by Marital Status & Department
- Single employees show higher attrition compared to Married and Divorced
- Sales department has consistently higher attrition across marital statuses

### Attrition % by Business Travel & Gender
- Employees who **Travel Frequently** have the highest attrition
- Attrition reduces significantly for employees with **Non-Travel** roles

### Attrition % by Job Satisfaction
- Higher attrition observed at **low job satisfaction levels**
- Attrition decreases as job satisfaction improves

### Attrition % by Age
- Younger employees show higher attrition
- Attrition stabilizes in mid-age groups and slightly increases at higher age levels

### Attrition % by Education Field
- Human Resources and Technical Degree fields show higher attrition
- Medical and Other fields show comparatively lower attrition

### Attrition % by Overtime
- Employees working **Overtime = Yes** have significantly higher attrition
- Overtime is a major attrition driver

---

## Job Role Analysis
The dashboard includes a detailed job role table showing:
- Employee Count
- Average Age
- Average Job Satisfaction
- Gender Ratio (Male % / Female %)
- Overtime %
- Average Salary

This helps identify job roles with high attrition risk and low job satisfaction.
